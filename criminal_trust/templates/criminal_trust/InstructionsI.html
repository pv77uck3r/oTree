Instructions

In this part of the experiment you will be randomly paired with one other participant who is your counterpart for this part of the experiment. You will not be paired with this participant in any other part of the experiment today.

You and your counterpart have each been given an initial 10 quarters (10 x $0.25).

You will decide how many of your 10 quarters you will send to your counterpart, if any. Any quarters you send will be tripled by the experimenter, and then the tripled number of quarters will be given to your counterpart. For instance if you choose to send 2 quarters to him/her, then the experimenter would triple those 2 quarters and he/she would receive 6 quarters (2x3=6). For another example, if you choose to send 8 quarters to him/her, then the experimenter would triple those 8 quarters and he/she would receive 24 quarters (8x3=24).

Your counterpart’s decision is how many of the quarters that he/she receives that they would like to send back to you. He/she is permitted to send back as many or as few as they would like. However, they may not send back any of their initial 10 quarters and the quarters are not multiplied on their way back to you.

For example, if you send your counterpart 2 quarters, he/she would receive 6 quarters (2x3 = 6). His/her decision would be whether to send 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6 quarters back to you. The total number of quarters you would have is the exact amount your counterpart sent back to you plus the remaining 8 quarters from your initial 10 quarters (10 – 2 = 8). Your counterpart would keep his/her initial 10 quarters plus the remaining quarters from the tripled amount that he/she received. For another example, if you choose to send 8 quarters to your counterpart, then he or she would receive 24 quarters (3x8 = 24). Your counterpart could then decide to send back as few as 0 quarters (in which case he/she would have their initial 10 quarters + 24 quarters – 0 quarters = 34 quarters and you would have your initial 10 quarters – 8 quarters + 0 quarters = 2 quarters) or he or she could send back as many as 24 quarters (in which case your counterpart would have their initial 10 quarters + 24 quarters – 24 quarters = 10 quarters and you would have your initial 10 quarters – 8 quarters + 24 quarters = 26 quarters).

For this part of the experiment, you will be randomly assigned to a counterpart who could be one of several “types”. In an interaction separate from their interaction with you the computer will randomly pair them with another participant. In that interaction, the computer will first divide $10 between your counterpart and this other participant. Your counterpart will be given an opportunity (that they are not forced to accept) to take money that belongs to their partner. This could be a small amount (between $0.10 and $1.00) a medium amount (between $1.00 and $2.00) or a large amount (between $2.00 and $3.00).

The decisions of other participants, in the role of a prosecutor and in the role of jurors, will determine whether those suspected of taking money from their partner are found guilty or not-guilty of having done so. Some truly innocent people may appear to be guilty and some truly guilty people may appear to be innocent. As a result, your possible counterparts are of different types: those found guilty of having taken a small amount of their partner’s money, those found guilty of having taken a medium amount, or those found guilty of having taken a large amount, those found not-guilty, and those never accused of such a theft. All participants found guilty of having taken money from another participant had to pay a monetary penalty as well.

Your decision is to tell us how many of your initial 10 quarters you will send to your counterpart in this part of the experiment.
