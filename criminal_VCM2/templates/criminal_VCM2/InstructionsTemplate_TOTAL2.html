Instructions

In this part of the experiment you will be randomly paired with two other participants who, with you, form your 3 person group for this part of the experiment. The decisions of you and your partners will determine your payoff and your partners' payoffs in this part of the experiment.

You and your two partners start with an endowment of 10 quarters. Your decision, and the decisions of your partners, is how many of your 10 quarters to put into your group's Group Account. The part of your endowment that you do not put into the Group Account will be put into your Private Account. Similarly, the part of your partners' endowment that they do not put into the Group Account are put into their respective private accounts. For every quarter you put into your Private Account, you will earn 1 quarter. Similarly, your partners will earn 1 quarter for each quarter they put into their respective Private Accounts.

For example, if you were to put 2 quarters into the Group Account, you will put 10 - 2 = 8 quarters into your Private Account. Thus, in this scenario, you would earn 8 quarters from your Private Account.

Each member will earn the same amount from what you give to the Group Account. On the other hand, you will also get a payoff from the other group members' contributions. Specifically, for each dollar held in the Group Account, everyone in your group earns \( \frac{1}{2} \) of a quarter. For example, if the sum of all contributions to the Group Account is 12 quarters, then each member of the group will earn \( 12 \times \frac{1}{2} = 6 \text{ quarters} \). If the sum of all contributions to the Group Account is 16 quarters, th en each member of the group will earn \( 16 \times \frac{1}{2} = 8 \text{ quarters} \) .

Your earnings for this part of hte experiment will be the amount you kept in your Private Account plus your earnings from the Group Account. Mathematically,

$$ \text{Payoff} = \text{Private Account} + \frac{1}{2} \times \text{Group Account} $$

For this part of the experiment, you will be randomly assigned two partners who could be one of several “types”. In an interaction separate from their interaction with you the computer will randomly pair them with another participant. In that interaction, the computer will first divide $10 between your counterpart and this other participant. Your counterpart will be given an opportunity (that they are not forced to accept) to take money that belongs to their partner. This could be any amount between $0.10 and $3.00.

The decisions of other participants, in the role of a prosecutor and in the role of jurors, will determine whether those suspected of taking money from their partner are found guilty or not-guilty of having done so. Some truly innocent people may appear to be guilty and some truly guilty people may appear to be innocent. As a result, your possible counterparts are of different types: those who have never been accused of a crime, those who have been accused, but never found guilty, and those who have been found guilty. All participants found guilty of having taken money from another participant had to pay a monetary penalty as well.

Your decision is to tell us how much of your 10 quarter endowment you would like to contribute to the Group Account. You can contribute in 1 quarter increments.

As previously stated, you will have 10 quarters at your disposal. You can put them into your Private Account or you can put them into the Group Account. Each subject has two types of decisions in this experiment, which will be referred to as "unconditional contribution" and "contribution list".

  1. Unconditional contribution: You will first decide how many of the 10 quarters you want to place into the Group Account.

  2. Contribution list: You will next decide to how to fill in a "contribution list" where you decide how many quarters you would like to contribute to the Group Account conditional on each possible contribution of the other group members.

    There will be several situations that describe the possible average contribution of the other group members to the Group Account. You simply have to select how many quarters you will contribute to the Group Account for each scenario. You must select a number everywhere you can. For example, you will have to indicate how much you will contribute if the other members of the group contribute an average of 0, 1, 2 quarters, etc. Again, you can select any whole number of quarters.

    After all participants in your group have made the above two sets of decisions, a random mechanism will select a single group member from every group. They will have their conditional contribution implemented and the rest of the group members will have their unconditional contributions implemented. You do not know whom will be selected in your group, so you must think carefully about both the conditional and unconditional contributions you will make.

Imagine that you are selected by the random mechanism. Imagine that the other two members of your group contribute an average of 10 quarters. The computer will then look in the contribution list for what you decided to contribute when the other members contributed and average of 10 quarters. Imagine you said you would contribute 4 quarters when the other members contributed an average of 10 quarters. Then the Group Account would have \( 2 \time 10 + 4 = 14 \text{ quarters} \). Since everyone gets a half quarter for every quarter in the Group Account, you would receive a 7 quarter return from the Group Account and a 6 quarter return from your Private Account (since 10 - 4 = 6), meaning, in total, you would have earned 13 quarters.

Now imagine you are not selected by the random mechanism. Assume your unconditional contribution to the Group Account is 6 quarters and the other unselected member of the group also contributed 6 quarters unconditionally. The computer will then look in the contribution list for what the selected group member decided to contribute when the other members contributed and average of 6 quarters. Imagine they contributed 8 quarters. Then the Group Account would total 6 + 6 + 8 = 20 quarters. Since everyone gets half of a quarter for every quarter in the Group Account, you would receive a 10 quarter return from the Group Account and a 4 quarter from your Private Account (since 10 - 6 = 4), meaning, in total, you would have earned 14 quarters.

The 2nd member of the group will always be the one that has their conditional contribution used in the payoff calculations. However, the computer randomly determines each members group ID number (1, 2, or 3) when forming the groups, and this number is never displayed to you or anyone else. Therefore the process for deciding whose conditional contribution will be used for determining payoffs is also random.
